1. GoT: Effective Graph-of-Thought Reasoning in Language Models

Source: S2:NAACL-HLT | Tags: LLM, Search Code: https://github Deep dive: Full analysis

Summary: Represent reasoning processes as a graph structure rather than a linear chain, where thought units are nodes and their relationships are edges, capturing the non-sequential nature of complex reasoning. Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning assumes a linear, sequential thought process, but human reasoning often involves non-linear connections, parallel considerations, and revisiting earlier thoughts—which CoT cannot model. Useful for: Mathematical reasoning tasks (e.g., AQUA-RAT)

Vault connections: Self-Consistency Decoding, Hierarchical Reasoning Model, Modeling Open-World Cognition as On-Demand Synthesis of Probabilistic Model

2. Learning to Reason via Program Generation, Emulation, and Search

Source: OpenReview:NeurIPS 2024 poster | Tags: LLM, Search Deep dive: Full analysis

Summary: Extend program synthesis to soft reasoning tasks by generating Python pseudo-programs with undefined leaf functions, then training the LM to emulate execution by filling in those gaps with its own knowledge. Standard program synthesis only works for algorithmic tasks with well-defined operations; it cannot handle reasoning tasks involving commonsense, moral judgment, or subjective interpretation that don’t map cleanly to executable code. Useful for: Commonsense reasoning tasks (e.g., Winograd schemas, social situations)

Vault connections: Agentic Reasoning Design Patterns, Reasoning Beyond Limits: Advances and Open Problems for LLMs, Self-Improving Language Models and the Evolution of Reasoning in AI

3. Can Custom Models Learn In-Context? An Exploration of Hybrid Architecture Performance on In-Context Learning Tasks

Source: OpenReview:Submitted to NeurIPS 2024 | Tags: LLM, Search, Embeddings Code: https://github.com/anonymousforneurips64/neurips2024-submission21757} Deep dive: Full analysis

Summary: A systematic empirical framework for evaluating how architectural mixing (GPT-2/LLaMA and LLaMA/Mamba block combinations) affects in-context learning performance on regression tasks, introducing the “ICL regression score” as a unified metric. Lack of understanding about which architectural components (MHA vs. other sequence transformers like Mamba) are critical for in-context learning capability, and how hybrid architectures compare to homogeneous designs. Useful for: Designing efficient ICL-optimized architectures for resource-constrained deployments

Vault connections: Learning without training: The implicit dynamics of in-context learning, Pignal, Linear Attention as In-Context Learners

4. VeSX: A Framework Featured by Verification, Self-Correction and In-context Learning for Web Automation Tasks

Source: OpenReview:Submitted to ICLR 2025 | Tags: LLM, RAG Deep dive: Full analysis

Summary: A three-component framework that enhances LLM-based web automation by verifying subtask completion against subgoals, hierarchically correcting planning and execution errors through reflection and replanning, and improving in-context learning via trajectory partitioning and metadata-enriched exemplar retrieval. Addresses the infeasibility of subtasks in LLM-driven web automation where traditional planning-execution workflows fail due to incorrect plans, failed executions, or inability to learn from past trajectories effectively. Useful for: Web automation and browser-based task completion (form filling, navigation, data extraction)

Vault connections: Promptomatix: An Automatic Prompt Optimization Framework for Large Language Model, A Survey of Context Engineering for Large Language Model, Dynamic Tool Invocation

5. A Field Guide to Automatic Evaluation of LLM-Generated Summaries

Source: S2:Annual International ACM SIGIR Conference on Research and Development in Information Retrieval | Tags: LLM, RecSys Deep dive: Full analysis

Summary: A systematic approach to evaluating LLM-generated summaries using a combination of traditional NLP metrics, LLM-as-judge methods, and specialized hallucination detection, structured around dimensions like faithfulness, relevance, coherence, and fluency. Traditional metrics (ROUGE, BLEU) only capture lexical overlap and miss semantic quality, while purely LLM-based evaluation lacks reliability and standardization. Useful for: Production LLM summarization systems requiring continuous quality monitoring without per-instance human review

Vault connections: LLM-based Pseudo-Label Generation and Multi-Metric Evaluation, G-Eval Simply Explained – LLM-as-a-judge for LLM evaluation, Large Language Model Enhanced Recommender Systems: A Survey

6. ReNeuIR at SIGIR 2024: The Third Workshop on Reaching Efficiency in Neural Information Retrieval

Source: S2:Annual International ACM SIGIR Conference on Research and Development in Information Retrieval | Tags: LLM, Search, RAG Deep dive: Full analysis

Summary: A standardized framework for measuring and comparing efficiency metrics (time, space, resource, sample, and energy) alongside effectiveness in neural information retrieval systems, addressing the lack of fair comparability due to varying hardware, software, and experimental settings. Modern IR evaluations focus heavily on effectiveness (relevance, ranking quality) while neglecting efficiency, making it impossible to fairly compare the cost-benefit ratio of different neural retrieval methods across different experimental setups and hardware configurations. Useful for: Comparing dense retrieval models vs. sparse retrieval methods on production constraints

Vault connections: Self-Retrieval: End-to-End Information Retrieval with One Large Language Model, Hybrid Evaluation: Precision, Recall, User Satisfaction Triad, Collapse of Dense Retriever: Takeaways and Techniques

7. CoDa: Constrained Generation based Data Augmentation for Low-Resource NLP

Source: S2:NAACL-HLT | Tags: LLM, RAG Code: https://github.com/Sreyan88/CoDa Deep dive: Full analysis

Summary: Generate synthetic training data for low-resource NLP tasks by extracting simple constraints from existing instances and prompting instruction-following LLMs to create new examples that satisfy those constraints. Low-resource NLP tasks lack sufficient training data, and existing augmentation methods either require expensive fine-tuning, produce biased outputs, or fail to provide explicit control over the generation process. Useful for: Low-resource classification tasks with limited labeled data

Vault connections: Drama: Diverse Augmentation from Large Language Models to Smaller Dense Retrievers, Dataset Featurization, Large Language Model for Synthetic Training Data in Dense retrieval

8. Using tournaments to calculate AUROC for zero-shot classification with LLMs

Source: S2:Findings of ACL. EMNLP. Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing | Tags: LLM, RecSys, RAG Deep dive: Full analysis

Summary: Convert zero-shot binary classification into pairwise instance comparisons, then use the Elo rating system to rank instances and compute AUROC, enabling proper evaluation of LLMs without requiring probability calibration or modifiable decision boundaries. LLMs lack calibrated probabilities and adjustable decision thresholds for zero-shot classification, making it impossible to fairly compare them to supervised classifiers using standard metrics like AUROC that require ranking all instances by confidence. Useful for: Zero-shot classification evaluation where LLMs replace traditional classifiers

Vault connections: Zero-Shot Generalization Scaling in LLM Dense Retrieval, Consolidating Ranking and Relevance Predictions of Large Language Models, Large Language Models for recommendation system

2. Reshape and Adapt for Output Quantization (RAOQ): Quantization-aware Training for In-memory Computing Systems

Summary: Mitigate ADC quantization errors in in-memory computing systems through activation-shifting and weight-reshaping to control distribution statistics, combined with bit augmentation during quantization-aware training to improve model tolerance. Analog-to-digital converters (ADCs) in in-memory computing (IMC) systems use limited precision for efficiency, causing substantial quantization errors in neural network outputs that degrade accuracy. Useful for: Edge AI deployment on in-memory computing hardware with constrained ADC precision
