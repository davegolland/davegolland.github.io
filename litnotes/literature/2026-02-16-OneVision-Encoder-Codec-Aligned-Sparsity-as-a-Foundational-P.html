Abstract

Hypothesis. Artificial general intelligence is, at its core, a compression problem. Effective compression demands resonance: deep learning scales best when its architecture aligns with the fundamental structure of the data. These are the fundamental principles. Yet, modern vision architectures have strayed from these truths: visual signals are highly redundant, while discriminative information, the surprise, is sparse. Current models process dense pixel grids uniformly, wasting vast compute on static background rather than focusing on the predictive residuals that define motion and meaning. We argue that to solve visual understanding, we must align our architectures with the information-theoretic principles of video, i.e., Codecs. Method. OneVision-Encoder encodes video by compressing predictive visual structure into semantic meaning. By adopting Codec Patchification, OV-Encoder abandons uniform computation to focus exclusively on the 3.1%-25% of regions rich in signal entropy. To unify spatial and temporal reasoning under irregular token layouts, OneVision-Encoder employs a shared 3D RoPE and is trained with a large-scale cluster discrimination objective over more than one million semantic concepts, jointly capturing object permanence and motion dynamics. Evidence. The results validate our core hypothesis: efficiency and accuracy are not a trade-off; they are positively correlated. When integrated into LLM, it consistently outperforms strong vision backbones such as Qwen3-ViT and SigLIP2 across 16 image, video, and document understanding benchmarks, despite using substantially fewer visual tokens and pretraining data. Notably, on video understanding tasks, OV-Encoder achieves an average improvement of 4.1% over Qwen3-ViT. Codec-aligned, patch-level sparsity is a foundational principle, enabling OV-Encoder as a scalable engine for next-generation visual generalists.

Codec-Aligned Sparsity (OneVision-Encoder)

Core Idea: Align vision encoder architecture with video codec principles by processing only high-entropy regions (3.1%-25% of patches) where predictive residuals concentrate, rather than uniformly processing all pixels.

Problem It Solves: Current vision transformers waste computation on redundant, static background regions instead of focusing on sparse, information-rich areas where motion and semantic changes occur. This creates an architectural misalignment with the fundamental information-theoretic structure of visual data.

How It Works:

  1. Codec Patchification: Decompose video frames into patches and identify regions with high signal entropy (predictive residuals) similar to I-frames/P-frames in video codecs, discarding 75-97% of redundant patches
  2. Irregular Token Layout Processing: Apply 3D Rotary Position Embeddings (RoPE) shared across spatial and temporal dimensions to handle non-uniform token distributions while preserving geometric relationships
  3. Large-Scale Cluster Discrimination: Train with contrastive objective over 1M+ semantic concepts to jointly learn object permanence and motion dynamics on the sparse token set

Architecture:

flowchart LR
    A[Video Frames] --> B[Codec Patchification]
    B --> C[Sparse Tokens<br/>3.1%-25%]
    C --> D[3D RoPE Encoding]
    D --> E[Cluster Discrimination<br/>1M+ concepts]
    E --> F[Semantic Embeddings]

Where It’s Useful:

  • Video understanding tasks (4.1% improvement over dense baselines)
  • Real-time video processing systems requiring low latency
  • Multimodal LLMs where vision token budget constrains context length
  • Document understanding with mixed static/dynamic content

Limitations / Caveats:

  • Requires careful tuning of entropy threshold to balance sparsity vs. information retention
  • May struggle with scenes where semantic information is uniformly distributed (e.g., texture classification)
  • Codec-style decomposition assumes temporal redundancy exists (less effective on single images)
  • Irregular token layouts may complicate integration with architectures assuming dense grids

Internal Use Cases:

  • Video recommendation ranking: Extract sparse motion/scene-change features for behavioral signals without processing full frame sequences
  • Content moderation: Focus compute budget on high-change regions where policy violations occur (faces, actions, text overlays)
  • Multimodal LLM serving: Reduce vision token count by 4-32× while maintaining accuracy, enabling longer context windows for RAG applications
  • Thumbnail selection: Identify high-entropy frames as candidates for video previews using codec residual magnitude
