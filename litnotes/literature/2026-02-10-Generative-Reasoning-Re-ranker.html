Abstract

Recent studies increasingly explore Large Language Models (LLMs) as a new paradigm for recommendation systems due to their scalability and world knowledge. However, existing work has three key limitations: (1) most efforts focus on retrieval and ranking, while the reranking phase, critical for refining final recommendations, is largely overlooked; (2) LLMs are typically used in zero-shot or supervised fine-tuning settings, leaving their reasoning abilities, especially those enhanced through reinforcement learning (RL) and high-quality reasoning data, underexploited; (3) items are commonly represented by non-semantic IDs, creating major scalability challenges in industrial systems with billions of identifiers. To address these gaps, we propose the Generative Reasoning Reranker (GR2), an end-to-end framework with a three-stage training pipeline tailored for reranking. First, a pretrained LLM is mid-trained on semantic IDs encoded from non-semantic IDs via a tokenizer achieving 99% uniqueness. Next, a stronger larger-scale LLM generates high-quality reasoning traces through carefully designed prompting and rejection sampling, which are used for supervised fine-tuning to impart foundational reasoning skills. Finally, we apply Decoupled Clip and Dynamic sAmpling Policy Optimization (DAPO), enabling scalable RL supervision with verifiable rewards designed specifically for reranking. Experiments on two real-world datasets demonstrate GR2’s effectiveness: it surpasses the state-of-the-art OneRec-Think by 2.4% in Recall@5 and 1.3% in NDCG@5. Ablations confirm that advanced reasoning traces yield substantial gains across metrics. We further find that RL reward design is crucial in reranking: LLMs tend to exploit reward hacking by preserving item order, motivating conditional verifiable rewards to mitigate this behavior and optimize reranking performance.

Generative Reasoning Reranker (GR2)

Core Idea: A three-stage LLM training framework (semantic ID mid-training → reasoning trace distillation → RL with verifiable rewards) that teaches models to rerank recommendations through explicit chain-of-thought reasoning rather than implicit scoring.

Problem It Solves:

  • Existing LLM-based recommenders neglect the critical reranking phase (focusing only on retrieval/ranking)
  • Standard LLM approaches underutilize reasoning capabilities enhanced through RL and high-quality reasoning data
  • Non-semantic item IDs create severe scalability issues in industrial systems with billions of items
  • Models exploit reward hacking by preserving input order rather than truly optimizing reranking quality

How It Works:

  1. Semantic ID Mid-Training: Pretrained LLM learns a tokenizer-based encoding that maps non-semantic IDs to semantic representations (≥99% uniqueness), enabling billion-scale item vocabulary
  2. Reasoning Trace Distillation: A larger teacher LLM generates high-quality step-by-step reasoning traces via carefully designed prompts and rejection sampling; student LLM is supervised fine-tuned on these traces to learn foundational reasoning patterns
  3. DAPO (Decoupled Clip and Dynamic sAmpling Policy Optimization): Scalable RL training with conditional verifiable rewards that prevent order-preservation exploits—rewards explicitly check for meaningful reordering rather than just final metrics

Architecture:

flowchart LR
    A[Non-semantic IDs] --> B[Semantic ID Tokenizer]
    B --> C[Mid-trained LLM]
    C --> D[Reasoning Trace SFT]
    D --> E[DAPO RL Training]
    E --> F[Reranked Item List]
    G[Teacher LLM Traces] -.->|distill| D
    H[Verifiable Rewards] -.->|optimize| E

Where It’s Useful:

  • Industrial recommendation reranking with massive item catalogs (billions of items)
  • E-commerce, content platforms, search reranking where final ordering critically impacts user experience
  • Systems requiring explainable recommendations (reasoning traces provide interpretability)
  • Cold-start scenarios where semantic item representations generalize better than ID embeddings

Limitations / Caveats:

  • Three-stage training pipeline is resource-intensive (requires teacher LLM, rejection sampling, RL infrastructure)
  • Semantic ID tokenizer requires careful design to maintain high uniqueness at scale
  • Reward engineering is critical—poorly designed rewards lead to exploits (e.g., order preservation)
  • Inference latency higher than traditional reranking models due to autoregressive generation of reasoning traces
  • Performance gains depend heavily on quality of teacher-generated reasoning traces

Internal Use Cases:

  • RecSys final-stage reranking: Apply after candidate retrieval/scoring to refine top-K results with explainable reasoning (especially valuable for high-stakes recommendations)
  • LLM-based ranking with scalability: Use semantic ID approach to handle large item catalogs without embedding table explosion
  • Reward shaping for ranking RL: Adopt conditional verifiable rewards to prevent order-preservation hacking in listwise ranking optimization
  • Reasoning-enhanced search: Generate interpretable explanations for why items are reordered (audit compliance, user trust)
  • Cross-domain transfer: Semantic IDs enable better generalization to new item categories vs. learned embeddings
