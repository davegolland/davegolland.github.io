Abstract

Safety validation for Level 4 autonomous vehicles (AVs) is currently bottlenecked by the inability to scale the detection of rare, high-risk long-tail scenarios using traditional rule-based heuristics. We present Deep-Flow, an unsupervised framework for safety-critical anomaly detection that utilizes Optimal Transport Conditional Flow Matching (OT-CFM) to characterize the continuous probability density of expert human driving behavior. Unlike standard generative approaches that operate in unstable, high-dimensional coordinate spaces, Deep-Flow constrains the generative process to a low-rank spectral manifold via a Principal Component Analysis (PCA) bottleneck. This ensures kinematic smoothness by design and enables the computation of the exact Jacobian trace for numerically stable, deterministic log-likelihood estimation. To resolve multi-modal ambiguity at complex junctions, we utilize an Early Fusion Transformer encoder with lane-aware goal conditioning, featuring a direct skip-connection to the flow head to maintain intent-integrity throughout the network. We introduce a kinematic complexity weighting scheme that prioritizes high-energy maneuvers (quantified via path tortuosity and jerk) during the simulation-free training process. Evaluated on the Waymo Open Motion Dataset (WOMD), our framework achieves an AUC-ROC of 0.766 against a heuristic golden set of safety-critical events. More significantly, our analysis reveals a fundamental distinction between kinematic danger and semantic non-compliance. Deep-Flow identifies a critical predictability gap by surfacing out-of-distribution behaviors, such as lane-boundary violations and non-normative junction maneuvers, that traditional safety filters overlook. This work provides a mathematically rigorous foundation for defining statistical safety gates, enabling objective, data-driven validation for the safe deployment of autonomous fleets.

Deep-Flow: Manifold-Constrained Optimal Transport Conditional Flow Matching (OT-CFM)

Core Idea: Model the continuous probability density of expert driving behavior by training a flow-matching generative model constrained to a low-rank PCA spectral manifold, then use exact log-likelihood scores as anomaly signals for safety-critical event detection.

Problem It Solves: Traditional rule-based heuristics cannot scale to rare long-tail driving scenarios; standard generative models operating in raw high-dimensional coordinate space suffer from kinematic instability, intractable Jacobian computation, and multi-modal ambiguity at complex junctions.

How It Works:

  1. PCA Spectral Bottleneck: Raw trajectory coordinates are projected onto a low-rank spectral manifold via PCA, enforcing kinematic smoothness by construction and collapsing dimensionality to a space where the Jacobian trace is analytically exact (no stochastic estimation needed).
  2. OT-CFM Training: A simulation-free flow-matching objective learns a deterministic vector field transporting a source Gaussian to the manifold-projected expert trajectory distribution; Optimal Transport couplings straighten flow paths, reducing training variance. A kinematic complexity weighting scheme up-weights high-jerk, high-tortuosity samples to prioritize rare energetic maneuvers.
  3. Conditioned Flow Inference: At inference, an Early Fusion Transformer encoder ingests scene context (agent history + lane graph) and produces a goal-conditioned latent with a direct skip-connection into the flow head, preserving intent signal. Log-likelihood under the learned density is computed exactly via the manifold Jacobian and used as the anomaly score — low likelihood = out-of-distribution = safety-critical flag.

Architecture:

flowchart LR
    A[Agent History\n+ Lane Graph] --> B[Early Fusion\nTransformer Encoder]
    B --> C[Goal-Conditioned\nLatent]
    C -- skip-connection --> F
    A --> D[PCA Spectral\nBottleneck]
    D --> E[Manifold\nCoordinates]
    E --> F[OT-CFM\nFlow Head]
    F --> G[Vector Field\n/ Log-Likelihood]
    G --> H[Anomaly Score]

Where It’s Useful:

  • Safety validation and statistical gating for Level 4 autonomous vehicle fleets
  • Unsupervised out-of-distribution detection in any sequential, trajectory-structured domain (robotics, UAV path planning)
  • Replacing hand-crafted heuristic filters with learned density thresholds in simulation pipelines

Limitations / Caveats:

  • PCA bottleneck rank is a fixed hyperparameter; too low a rank discards semantically meaningful variance, too high reintroduces instability — sensitive to dataset characteristics
  • AUC-ROC of 0.766 against a heuristic golden set means the method is not yet a drop-in replacement for curated safety oracles; golden set quality directly caps measured performance
  • Kinematic complexity weighting requires defining tortuosity/jerk thresholds, reintroducing domain priors the framework aims to avoid
  • Evaluated on a single dataset (WOMD); generalization to other sensor modalities or geographic domains is unverified

Internal Use Cases:

  • Ranking/RecSys: The OT-CFM density estimation pattern is directly applicable to session-sequence anomaly detection — model normal user engagement trajectories on a spectral manifold and flag low-likelihood sessions as fraud, bot traffic, or distribution shift without labeled anomaly data
  • LLM evaluation: Apply manifold-constrained flow matching to token-probability trajectories across decoding steps to detect out-of-distribution generations (hallucinations, jailbreaks) as low-likelihood sequences under a learned “safe behavior” density
  • Data quality gating: Use exact log-likelihood scores as a simulation-free filter to automatically surface and up-weight rare but high-signal training examples in fine-tuning pipelines, analogous to the kinematic complexity weighting scheme
