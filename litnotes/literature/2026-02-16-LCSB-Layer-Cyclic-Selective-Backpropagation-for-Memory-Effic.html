LCSB: Layer-Cyclic Selective Backpropagation for Memory-Efficient On-Device LLM Fine-Tuning

Source: arXiv:cs.CL Published: 2026-02-13 Rank: #1 (score: 1.00, max_sim: 0.82) Reading time: ~15 min Tags: LLM, Transformer, Early-Exit, NSA

Abstract

Memory-efficient backpropagation (MeBP) has enabled first-order fine-tuning of large language models (LLMs) on mobile devices with less than 1GB memory. However, MeBP requires backward computation through all transformer layers at every step, where weight decompression alone accounts for 32—42% of backward time. We propose Layer-Cyclic Selective Backpropagation (LCSB), which computes gradients for only a subset of layers per step. Our key insight is that residual connections guarantee gradient flow through identity paths, while AdamW momentum provides implicit updates for non-selected layers. We interpret LCSB as Block Coordinate Descent on the LoRA parameter space, providing theoretical justification for convergence. LCSB achieves up to 1.40 speedup with less than 2% quality degradation across five models and three tasks. Surprisingly, in 4-bit quantized settings, LCSB exhibits superior stability: a 3B model that completely diverges under full backpropagation converges smoothly with LCSB, suggesting an implicit regularization effect from selective gradient computation.

Key Techniques

  • Layer-Cyclic Selective Backpropagation (LCSB)

Layer-Cyclic Selective Backpropagation (LCSB)

Core Idea: Compute gradients for only a subset of transformer layers per training step while relying on residual connections for gradient flow and AdamW momentum for implicit updates to non-selected layers.

Problem It Solves: Memory-efficient backpropagation (MeBP) enables on-device LLM fine-tuning but still requires backward computation through all layers at every step, with weight decompression consuming 32-42% of backward time—a critical bottleneck for mobile devices with <1GB memory.

How It Works:

  1. Layer selection: At each training step, select only a cyclic subset of transformer layers for gradient computation (e.g., layers 0-7 at step 1, layers 8-15 at step 2, etc.)
  2. Selective backpropagation: Compute gradients and update LoRA parameters only for selected layers; skip gradient computation for non-selected layers entirely
  3. Gradient flow preservation: Residual connections provide identity paths that allow gradients to flow through non-selected layers without explicit computation
  4. Implicit updates: AdamW momentum accumulates gradient information across steps, providing implicit parameter updates for layers not selected in the current step

Architecture:

flowchart TB
    A[Forward Pass: All Layers] --> B{Step t mod K}
    B -->|Cycle 0| C[Backward: Layers 0 to L/K]
    B -->|Cycle 1| D[Backward: Layers L/K to 2L/K]
    B -->|Cycle K-1| E[Backward: Layers (K-1)L/K to L]
    C --> F[Update LoRA params for selected layers only]
    D --> F
    E --> F
    F --> G[AdamW momentum provides implicit updates to non-selected layers]

Where It’s Useful:

  • On-device LLM fine-tuning on mobile/edge devices with severe memory constraints (<1GB)
  • LoRA-based parameter-efficient fine-tuning where gradient computation dominates training time
  • 4-bit quantized model training where full backpropagation exhibits instability

Limitations / Caveats:

  • Theoretical convergence relies on Block Coordinate Descent assumptions (convexity, coordinate-wise smoothness) that may not fully hold for non-convex neural network optimization
  • Quality degradation up to 2% observed in some settings compared to full backpropagation
  • Cyclic scheduling strategy and cycle length K require tuning per model/task
  • Effectiveness depends on AdamW momentum providing sufficient implicit updates; may not generalize to other optimizers

Internal Use Cases:

  • Enable federated learning or personalized fine-tuning of recommendation models on user devices without requiring high-memory hardware
  • Accelerate multi-task LoRA training for ranking models by cycling through task-specific adapter layers
  • Reduce training infrastructure costs for continuous model updates by trading modest quality for significant speedup (1.4× demonstrated)
  • Explore implicit regularization effects for stabilizing low-bit quantized LLM training in production systems
