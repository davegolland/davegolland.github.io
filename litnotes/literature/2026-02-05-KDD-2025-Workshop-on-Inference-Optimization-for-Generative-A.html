Abstract

The demand for efficient Large Language Model (LLM) inference has surged with the rising adoption of Generative AI (GenAI) applications, particularly in areas such as agents and retrieval-augmented generation. Efficient inference serves two crucial purposes: it enables the deployment of LLM-centered applications that address critical business needs, while also facilitating rapid experimentation for researchers to extract valuable insights and new understandings. However, despite the field’s rapid advancement and interdisciplinary nature, there remains a limited exchange of ideas and methodologies between production-facing practitioners and researchers seeking to experiment with new GenAI concepts quickly. To bridge this gap, we are introducing the first KDD workshop on Inference Optimization for Generative AI. Our goal is to create a collaborative platform where researchers and practitioners working across various use cases and stacks of efficient inference can come together to exchange research ideas, establish connections between different disciplines, and identify challenges and research questions that will shape future work.

Inference Optimization for Generative AI (KDD Workshop Framework)

Core Idea: Create a collaborative interdisciplinary platform bridging production-oriented practitioners and researchers to exchange methodologies and identify challenges in efficient Large Language Model inference for GenAI applications.

Problem It Solves: Despite rapid advancement in LLM inference optimization, there is limited knowledge exchange between practitioners deploying production systems and researchers experimenting with new GenAI concepts, leading to siloed development and missed opportunities for cross-pollination of ideas.

How It Works:

  1. Knowledge Exchange - Brings together researchers and practitioners working across different layers of the inference stack (model architecture, serving infrastructure, application layer)
  2. Cross-Disciplinary Connection - Establishes links between diverse use cases (agents, retrieval-augmented generation, real-time applications) and optimization approaches (quantization, distillation, batching strategies)
  3. Research Agenda Formation - Collaboratively identifies bottlenecks, challenges, and open problems that will guide future research directions in inference efficiency

Where It’s Useful:

  • Deploying LLM-centered applications with critical latency/cost constraints
  • Rapid prototyping and experimentation with novel GenAI architectures
  • Agent systems requiring real-time inference
  • Retrieval-augmented generation pipelines at scale

Limitations / Caveats:

  • Workshop format provides exchange opportunities but does not directly produce technical solutions
  • Success depends on active participation from both industry and academia
  • Gap between research insights and production deployment may still require significant engineering effort

Internal Use Cases:

  • Optimizing inference for recommendation model serving (latency-sensitive ranking)
  • Scaling RAG systems for search and discovery features
  • Developing efficient agent architectures for personalized user interactions
  • Knowledge distillation from large rankers to deployable dense retrievers (connects to Drama paper in vault)
