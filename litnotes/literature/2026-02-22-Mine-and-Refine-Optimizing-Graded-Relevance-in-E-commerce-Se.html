Mine and Refine: Optimizing Graded Relevance in E-commerce Search Retrieval

Mine and Refine: Optimizing Graded Relevance in E-commerce Search Retrieval

Source: arXiv:cs.IR Published: 2026-02-19 Rank: #11 (score: 0.93, max_sim: 0.85) Reading time: ~0 min Tags: Uncategorized

Abstract

We propose a two-stage “Mine and Refine” contrastive training framework for semantic text embeddings to enhance multi-category e-commerce search retrieval. Large scale e-commerce search demands embeddings that generalize to long tail, noisy queries while adhering to scalable supervision compatible with product and policy constraints. A practical challenge is that relevance is often graded: users accept substitutes or complements beyond exact matches, and production systems benefit from clear separation of similarity scores across these relevance strata for stable hybrid blending and thresholding. To obtain scalable policy consistent supervision, we fine-tune a lightweight LLM on human annotations under a three-level relevance guideline and further reduce residual noise via engagement driven auditing. In Stage 1, we train a multilingual Siamese two-tower retriever with a label aware supervised contrastive objective that shapes a robust global semantic space. In Stage 2, we mine hard samples via ANN and re-annotate them with the policy aligned LLM, and introduce a multi-class extension of circle loss that explicitly sharpens similarity boundaries between relevance levels, to further refine and enrich the embedding space. Robustness is additionally improved through additive spelling augmentation and synthetic query generation. Extensive offline evaluations and production A/B tests show that our framework improves retrieval relevance and delivers statistically significant gains in engagement and business impact.

Key Techniques

  • Two-stage “Mine and Refine” contrastive framework
  • Multilingual Siamese two-tower retriever with supervised contrastive learning
  • Hard negative mining via ANN with multi-class circle loss
  • Synthetic query generation and spelling augmentation

Mine and Refine Two-Stage Graded Contrastive Training

Core Idea: A two-stage embedding training framework that first builds a global semantic space with label-aware contrastive learning, then iteratively mines hard boundary samples via Approximate Nearest Neighbor search and sharpens inter-relevance-level similarity margins using a multi-class extension of circle loss.

Problem It Solves: Standard bi-encoder training collapses graded relevance signals (exact match, substitute, complement) into an undifferentiated similarity score, making downstream hybrid score blending and relevance thresholding unstable and requiring embeddings that generalize to long-tail, noisy e-commerce queries at scale.

How It Works:

  1. LLM Supervision Bootstrap: Fine-tune a lightweight LLM on human-annotated query-product pairs under a three-level relevance guideline; run engagement-driven auditing to remove residual label noise, producing a scalable, policy-consistent annotation oracle.
  2. Stage 1 — Global Space Shaping: Train a multilingual Siamese two-tower retriever using a label-aware supervised contrastive objective; augment training data with additive spelling perturbations and synthetic query generation to improve robustness on noisy/long-tail inputs.
  3. Stage 2 — Hard Boundary Refinement: Mine hard samples (near-boundary pairs across relevance strata) via ANN search in the Stage 1 embedding space; re-annotate them with the policy-aligned LLM; retrain with a multi-class circle loss extension that explicitly maximizes similarity margin between each pair of relevance levels rather than treating all negatives uniformly.

Architecture:

flowchart LR
    A[Raw Query-Product Pairs] --> B[Fine-tuned LLM Annotator\n3-level relevance + auditing]
    B --> C[Stage 1: Siamese Two-Tower\nLabel-Aware Sup-Con Loss\n+ Spell Aug + SynQ Gen]
    C --> D[ANN Hard Sample Mining\nfrom Stage 1 embedding space]
    D --> E[LLM Re-annotation\nof boundary pairs]
    E --> F[Stage 2: Multi-Class\nCircle Loss Refinement]
    F --> G[Refined Embedding Space\nwith explicit relevance strata]

Where It’s Useful:

  • Large-scale e-commerce semantic retrieval where relevance is graded (exact, substitute, complement)
  • Multilingual product search requiring policy-consistent, scalable label generation
  • Hybrid retrieval systems where ANN score distributions must be stable for blending/thresholding
  • Any domain with noisy labels where iterative hard-negative mining can surface boundary ambiguities

Limitations / Caveats:

  • Two-stage pipeline increases training complexity and requires maintaining an LLM annotation oracle at mining time, adding inference cost between stages
  • Hard negative mining quality is bounded by Stage 1 embedding quality — a poor initial space will surface uninformative boundaries
  • Three-level relevance taxonomy may not capture all nuances (e.g., accessory vs. complement); taxonomy design becomes a critical upstream dependency
  • Multi-class circle loss hyperparameters (margin, scale per relevance pair) require careful tuning and may not transfer across product verticals

Internal Use Cases:

  • RecSys candidate retrieval: Apply graded circle loss to separate “purchased,” “clicked,” and “impressed-but-ignored” items rather than binary positive/negative, improving top-of-funnel recall precision
  • LLM-assisted ranking data pipelines: Use the LLM annotation + engagement auditing loop as a template for cheaply generating graded relevance labels for any query-item ranking task
  • Embedding quality diagnosis: ANN-mined boundary pairs from Stage 1 serve as a natural stress-test set for evaluating where the embedding space conflates relevance levels — connects directly to the Semantic Certainty Assessment framework in vault notes
