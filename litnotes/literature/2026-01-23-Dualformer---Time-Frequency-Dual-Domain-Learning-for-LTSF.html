Dualformer: Time-Frequency Dual Domain Learning for Long-term Time Series Forecasting

Dualformer: Time-Frequency Dual Domain Learning for Long-term Time Series Forecasting

Source: arXiv:2601.15669 (cs.LG) Date: 2026-01-22 Score: 2.64 (max_sim=0.736, novel territory)

The Problem

Transformer-based LTSF models suffer from an inherent low-pass filtering effect — high-frequency information gets progressively attenuated across layers because frequency components propagate indiscriminately. This loses fine-grained temporal variations crucial for accurate forecasting.

Key Insight

Don’t treat all frequencies the same across layers. Instead:

  • Preserve high-frequency details in lower/earlier layers
  • Model low-frequency trends in deeper layers

This is a layer-wise perspective on frequency modeling that existing work ignores.

The Dualformer Architecture

Three components:

1. Dual-Branch Architecture

Concurrently models patterns in both time and frequency domains:

  • Time branch: captures local temporal dependencies
  • Frequency branch: captures global periodic patterns

2. Hierarchical Frequency Sampling

Different layers handle different frequency bands:

  • Lower layers → high-frequency bands (fine details)
  • Deeper layers → low-frequency bands (trends)

This prevents the low-pass filtering effect by giving high frequencies their own dedicated processing path.

3. Periodicity-Aware Weighting

Dynamically balances contributions from dual branches based on harmonic energy ratio of inputs:

  • High periodicity → weight frequency branch more
  • Low periodicity → weight time branch more

Theoretically grounded with a derived lower bound.

Results

Tested on 8 benchmarks. Particularly strong on:

  • Heterogeneous data (mixed patterns)
  • Weakly periodic data (where frequency-only approaches struggle)
