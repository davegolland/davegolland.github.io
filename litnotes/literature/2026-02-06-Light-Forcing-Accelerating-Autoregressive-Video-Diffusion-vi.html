Light Forcing: Accelerating Autoregressive Video Diffusion via Sparse Attention

Source: HuggingFace Published: 2026-02-04 Rank: #7 (score: 0.77, max_sim: 0.81) Reading time: ~0 min Tags: Block-Sparse, NSA

Abstract

Advanced autoregressive (AR) video generation models have improved visual fidelity and interactivity, but the quadratic complexity of attention remains a primary bottleneck for efficient deployment. While existing sparse attention solutions have shown promise on bidirectional models, we identify that applying these solutions to AR models leads to considerable performance degradation for two reasons: isolated consideration of chunk generation and insufficient utilization of past informative context. Motivated by these observations, we propose Light Forcing, the first sparse attention solution tailored for AR video generation models. It incorporates a Chunk-Aware Growth mechanism to quantitatively estimate the contribution of each chunk, which determines their sparsity allocation. This progressive sparsity increase strategy enables the current chunk to inherit prior knowledge in earlier chunks during generation. Additionally, we introduce a Hierarchical Sparse Attention to capture informative historical and local context in a coarse-to-fine manner. Such two-level mask selection strategy (\ie, frame and block level) can adaptively handle diverse attention patterns. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method outperforms existing sparse attention in quality (\eg, 84.5 on VBench) and efficiency (\eg, 1.2{sim}1.3times end-to-end speedup). Combined with FP8 quantization and LightVAE, Light Forcing further achieves a 2.3times speedup and 19.7,FPS on an RTX~5090 GPU. Code will be released at https://github.com/chengtao-lv/LightForcing{https://github.com/chengtao-lv/LightForcing}.

Light Forcing

Core Idea: A sparse attention framework for autoregressive video diffusion that progressively allocates sparsity across chunks using contribution estimation, combined with hierarchical frame-and-block-level context selection.

Problem It Solves: Existing sparse attention methods degrade performance on autoregressive (AR) video models because they (1) treat chunks in isolation without considering inter-chunk dependencies, and (2) fail to leverage informative past context that AR models accumulate during sequential generation.

How It Works:

  1. Chunk-Aware Growth: Quantitatively estimates each chunk’s contribution to the current generation step, allocating lower sparsity (more attention) to high-contribution chunks and progressively increasing sparsity as generation proceeds, enabling knowledge inheritance from earlier chunks.
  2. Hierarchical Sparse Attention: Applies a two-level mask selection strategy—first selecting important frames from historical context (coarse level), then selecting informative blocks within those frames and the local chunk (fine level)—to adaptively capture diverse attention patterns.
  3. Outputs sparse attention masks that maintain AR dependencies while reducing quadratic complexity, preserving visual fidelity and temporal coherence.

Architecture:

flowchart LR
    A[Video Chunks] --> B[Chunk Contribution Estimation]
    B --> C[Progressive Sparsity Allocation]
    A --> D[Frame-level Selection]
    D --> E[Block-level Selection]
    C --> F[Sparse Attention Mask]
    E --> F
    F --> G[Accelerated AR Generation]

Where It’s Useful:

  • Autoregressive video diffusion models requiring long-context generation
  • Real-time or interactive video synthesis applications (achieves 19.7 FPS on RTX 5090)
  • Deployment scenarios where quadratic attention complexity is prohibitive

Limitations / Caveats:

  • Specifically designed for AR models; benefits may not transfer to bidirectional architectures
  • Requires tuning of sparsity allocation schedules and hierarchical selection thresholds
  • Contribution estimation adds overhead, though amortized across generation steps

Internal Use Cases:

  • Video recommendation preview generation: accelerate AR-based video thumbnails or summaries at scale
  • Content understanding models: apply chunk-aware context selection to long video sequences in ranking features
  • Multi-modal LLM training: hierarchical sparse attention could extend to autoregressive image-text generation tasks
