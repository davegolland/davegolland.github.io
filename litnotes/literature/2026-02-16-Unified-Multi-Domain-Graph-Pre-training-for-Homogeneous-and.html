Unified Multi-Domain Graph Pre-training for Homogeneous and Heterogeneous Graphs via Domain-Specific Expert Encoding

Unified Multi-Domain Graph Pre-training for Homogeneous and Heterogeneous Graphs via Domain-Specific Expert Encoding

Source: arXiv:cs.LG Published: 2026-02-13 Rank: #4 (score: 0.99, max_sim: 0.83) Reading time: ~20 min Tags: Uncategorized

Abstract

Graph pre-training has achieved remarkable success in recent years, delivering transferable representations for downstream adaptation. However, most existing methods are designed for either homogeneous or heterogeneous graphs, thereby hindering unified graph modeling across diverse graph types. This separation contradicts real-world applications, where mixed homogeneous and heterogeneous graphs are ubiquitous, and distribution shifts between upstream pre-training and downstream deployment are common. In this paper, we empirically demonstrate that a balanced mixture of homogeneous and heterogeneous graph pre-training benefits downstream tasks and propose a unified multi-domain \textbf{G}raph \textbf{P}re-training method across \textbf{H}omogeneous and \textbf{H}eterogeneous graphs (). To address the lack of a unified encoder for homogeneous and heterogeneous graphs, we propose a Unified Multi-View Graph Construction that simultaneously encodes both without explicit graph-type-specific designs. To cope with the increased cross-domain distribution discrepancies arising from mixed graphs, we introduce domain-specific expert encoding. Each expert is independently pre-trained on a single graph to capture domain-specific knowledge, thereby shielding the pre-training encoder from the adverse effects of cross-domain discrepancies. For downstream tasks, we further design a Task-oriented Expert Fusion Strategy that adaptively integrates multiple experts based on their discriminative strengths. Extensive experiments on mixed graphs demonstrate that enables stable transfer across graph types and domains, significantly outperforming existing graph pre-training methods.

GPH² (Graph Pre-training across Homogeneous and Heterogeneous graphs)

Core Idea: A unified multi-domain graph pre-training framework that trains domain-specific experts independently on both homogeneous and heterogeneous graphs, then adaptively fuses them for downstream tasks to handle cross-graph-type and cross-domain distribution shifts.

Problem It Solves: Existing graph pre-training methods are tailored to either homogeneous or heterogeneous graphs exclusively, failing to leverage mixed graph types during pre-training and struggling with distribution shifts when the upstream pre-training graph type differs from downstream deployment graphs.

How It Works:

  1. Unified Multi-View Graph Construction: Transform both homogeneous and heterogeneous graphs into a unified representation by constructing multiple views (structural, semantic, attribute-based) without explicit graph-type-specific architectures, enabling a single encoder to process both graph types.
  2. Domain-Specific Expert Encoding: Pre-train separate expert encoders, each on a single source graph (domain), to capture domain-specific knowledge while isolating the model from adverse cross-domain distribution discrepancies during pre-training.
  3. Task-Oriented Expert Fusion: For downstream tasks, adaptively weight and combine representations from multiple pre-trained experts based on their discriminative power for the target task, measured via validation performance or attention mechanisms.

Where It’s Useful:

  • Cross-domain graph transfer learning where training and test graphs have different structures (e.g., molecular graphs → social networks)
  • Multi-task learning on mixed homogeneous and heterogeneous graph datasets
  • Zero-shot or few-shot graph learning scenarios with domain shift

Limitations / Caveats:

  • Requires training multiple expert models, increasing computational cost linearly with the number of source domains
  • Fusion strategy may require downstream task validation data to determine optimal expert weights, limiting true zero-shot applicability
  • Performance gain depends on diversity and relevance of source domains to the target task

Internal Use Cases:

  • Cross-platform RecSys: Pre-train experts on user-item graphs from different platforms (e.g., e-commerce, streaming, social media) with varying structures, then fuse for cold-start recommendations on new platforms
  • Multi-relational ranking: Handle heterogeneous knowledge graphs (entities with typed relations) and homogeneous user behavior graphs jointly for context-aware ranking
  • LLM-augmented graph reasoning: Combine graph-structured knowledge bases (heterogeneous) with citation/co-occurrence networks (homogeneous) to enhance retrieval-augmented generation
