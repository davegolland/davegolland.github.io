FlashVID: Efficient Video Large Language Models via Training-free Tree-based Spatiotemporal Token Merging

Source: arXiv:cs.CL Published: 2026-02-08 Rank: #2 (score: 0.82, max_sim: 0.81) Reading time: ~0 min Tags: NSA

Abstract

Although Video Large Language Models (VLLMs) have shown remarkable capabilities in video understanding, they are required to process high volumes of visual tokens, causing significant computational inefficiency. Existing VLLMs acceleration frameworks usually compress spatial and temporal redundancy independently, which overlooks the spatiotemporal relationships, thereby leading to suboptimal spatiotemporal compression. The highly correlated visual features are likely to change in spatial position, scale, orientation, and other attributes over time due to the dynamic nature of video. Building on this insight, we introduce FlashVID, a training-free inference acceleration framework for VLLMs. Specifically, FlashVID utilizes Attention and Diversity-based Token Selection (ADTS) to select the most representative tokens for basic video representation, then applies Tree-based Spatiotemporal Token Merging (TSTM) for fine-grained spatiotemporal redundancy elimination. Extensive experiments conducted on three representative VLLMs across five video understanding benchmarks demonstrate the effectiveness and generalization of our method. Notably, by retaining only 10% of visual tokens, FlashVID preserves 99.1% of the performance of LLaVA-OneVision. Consequently, FlashVID can serve as a training-free and plug-and-play module for extending long video frames, which enables a 10x increase in video frame input to Qwen2.5-VL, resulting in a relative improvement of 8.6% within the same computational budget. Code is available at https://github.com/Fanziyang-v/FlashVID.

FlashVID (Training-free Tree-based Spatiotemporal Token Merging)

Core Idea: Training-free inference acceleration for Video Large Language Models through spatiotemporal token compression that preserves video relationships by first selecting representative tokens, then merging spatiotemporally correlated features across frames.

Problem It Solves: Existing VLLMs require processing massive volumes of visual tokens, causing computational inefficiency. Prior compression methods treat spatial and temporal redundancy independently, missing spatiotemporal correlations where visual features change position, scale, and orientation over time.

How It Works:

  1. Attention and Diversity-based Token Selection (ADTS): Select the most representative visual tokens based on attention scores and diversity metrics to form a basic video representation
  2. Tree-based Spatiotemporal Token Merging (TSTM): Build a tree structure to identify and merge tokens that are correlated across both spatial and temporal dimensions, eliminating fine-grained redundancy while tracking how features evolve across frames
  3. Inference: Pass the compressed token set (as low as 10% of original) to the VLLM for video understanding tasks

Architecture:

flowchart LR
    A[Video Frames] --> B[Vision Encoder]
    B --> C[ADTS: Select Representative Tokens]
    C --> D[TSTM: Tree-based Merging]
    D --> E[Compressed Tokens 10%]
    E --> F[Language Model]
    F --> G[Video Understanding Output]

Where It’s Useful:

  • Long-form video understanding where computational budget is limited
  • Real-time video analysis applications requiring efficient inference
  • Extending context length for video-language models (enables 10x more frames)
  • Plug-and-play acceleration for existing VLLMs without retraining

Limitations / Caveats:

  • Performance degrades slightly at extreme compression ratios (10% retention = 99.1% performance, but further compression may hurt more)
  • Effectiveness depends on the existence of spatiotemporal redundancy in the video content
  • Tree construction overhead may not justify benefits for very short videos

Internal Use Cases:

  • Video recommendation systems: compress video representations for efficient similarity search and ranking
  • Multimodal content understanding: apply spatiotemporal merging to reduce token costs when processing video+text in feeds
  • Long-context video retrieval: enable indexing of longer videos within fixed compute budgets by compressing visual tokens before embedding
